Establishment of UCD Department

The UCD Dept was established in the year 1979 in erstwhile Visakhapatnam Municipal Corporation (VMC). The area of the city was 76.33 Sq.K.M and the population was 5.60 lakhs. There were 79 slums with 68,000 slum population.

The UNICEF supported the Visakhapatnam Municipal Corporation (VMC) in taking up Mother & Child Welfare programmes along with State Govt. programmes such as EWS housing and Environmental improvement Schemes in the Slums. The support from UNICEF continued till 1986. In 1988, based on the success of these programmes, the ODA/DFID (UK Govt.) also came forward to support the Visakhapatnam Slum Improvement Programme with help from State and Central Government.

The main objectives of Visakhapatnam Slum Improvement Programme are twofold: physical improvement and community development.

Under physical development:

  • Roads
  • Drainage System
  • Sanitation
  • Water Supply
  • Improvement of major streams
  • Construction of community buildings

Under community development:

  • Social Development
  • Economic Improvement
  • Improvement of literacy through Education
  • Improvement of health conditions

While implementing the programme, the city grew from 76.33 Sq.Km to 112 Sq.Km and from 5.60 lakhs to 12 lakhs population. Slums increased from 76 to 252 and slum population from 68,000 to 2.16 lakhs.

The project period was from 1988 to 1996, including the Chinnagadili habitat improvement Scheme I & II.
