RAJIV AWAS YOJANA

NOTES ON RAY FOR PLACING IN GVMC WEBSITE

1. Vision

  • Rajiv Awas Yojana (RAY) envisages a ‚ÄúSlum Free India‚Äù with inclusive and equitable cities in which every citizen has access to basic civic infrastructure, social amenities and decent shelter.

2. Mission

  • Bringing all existing slums, notified or non-notified (including recognised and identified) within the formal system and enabling them to avail the basic amenities that are available for the rest of the city/UA.
  • Redressing the failures of the formal system that lie behind the creation of slums by planning for affordable housing stock for the urban poor and initiating crucial policy changes required for facilitating the same.

3. Objectives

  • 3.1 Improving and provisioning of housing, basic civic infrastructure and social amenities in intervened slums.
  • 3.2 Enabling reforms to address some of the causes leading to creation of slums.
  • 3.3 Facilitating a supportive environment for expanding institutional credit linkages for the urban poor.
  • 3.4 Institutionalizing mechanisms for prevention of slums including creation of affordable housing stock.
  • 3.5 Strengthening institutional and human resource capacities at the Municipal, City and State levels through comprehensive capacity building and strengthening of resource networks.
  • 3.6 Empowering community by ensuring their participation at every stage of decision making through strengthening and nurturing Slum Dwellers‚Äô Association/Federations.

4. Scope

  • 4.1 RAY is to be implemented in a mission mode and will provide financial support to implementing agencies for housing and civic infrastructure in selected slums. Includes rental and transit housing, and O&M support.
  • 4.2 RAY will also support affordable housing stock creation via public-private partnership (PPP) under the AHP component.

5. Duration

  • The implementation phase of the scheme is from 2013 to 2022.

6. Coverage

  • 6.1 Scheme applies to all cities/UAs. Selection by States in consultation with Centre.
    • 6.1.1 Priority to cities/UAs with large slum populations.
    • 6.1.2 Preference for district HQs and culturally significant cities.
    • 6.1.3 Priority for cities with SC/ST/minority/vulnerable groups.
    • 6.1.4 States/UTs to consider their financial capacity.
  • 6.2 Applicable to all slums (notified or not), across all types of land ownership, including urbanized villages, homeless and pavement dwellers.

7. Redevelopment & Upgradation

  • Redevelopment: Complete redevelopment after demolition, with full infrastructure and housing.
  • Upgradation: Fill gaps in infrastructure/housing without complete demolition.

8. Important Points

  • 8.1 Transit housing may be proposed in the DPR if needed for displaced slum dwellers.
  • 8.2 For slum relocation, mobility and livelihood linkage should be emphasized. Infrastructure must be provided at the new site.

Types of Housing

  • 10.1 New Housing: For slum dwellers without pucca houses; 21‚Äì27 sqm units with 2 rooms, kitchen, toilet, and water.
  • 10.2 Incremental Housing: Improve existing units (add rooms, toilets). Consider vertical development if land is limited. Provide basic services.
  • 10.3 Rental Housing: For tenants, labourers, and homeless. Size 16‚Äì20 sqm (or 21‚Äì27 in some cases). States must define rent and O&M policies.
  • 10.4 Beneficiary-led Execution: Construction by beneficiaries. Funded in up to 4 installments:
    • 10% before construction
    • 30% at plinth level
    • 40% at roof level
    • 20% on completion (adjustable)

Executing Agencies

  • 11.1 Projects may be executed by ULBs or agencies like Housing Boards and Development Authorities.
  • 11.2 Community participation is essential from planning to post-implementation. Recommend forming slum dwellers' cooperatives/associations for ownership, rent, and maintenance responsibilities.
